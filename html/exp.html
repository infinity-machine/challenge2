Restaurant Career

Since 2010 I've been paying my rent by cooking, food running, bar-backing, serving, and just about anything else I can get my hands on in various restaurants across the twin cities. The most important thing I've learned is to keep moving, and never let a seemingly impossible workload slow your pace. I had no choice but to develop a work ethic that, in my mind, overqualifies me for just about anything else. Whether I'm serving 8 tables at once, or churning out a mountain of code, I do so at a pace that is both careful and competetive, and I never rest until the job is done.
